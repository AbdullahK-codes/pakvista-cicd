Karachi City of Lights

Pakistan's largest city and main port, alive day and night.

Clifton Beach

Clifton Beach is one of the city's most visited public spaces, drawing families and vendors along its long, sandy shoreline. Evenings are especially lively, with camel rides and street food stalls lining the promenade. The adjacent Dolmen City Mall and Sea View restaurants make it a convenient spot for both locals and tourists to spend an entire afternoon by the Arabian Sea.

Mohatta Palace

Built in 1927 as a summer residence, Mohatta Palace blends Mughal, Rajasthani, and European architectural styles using locally sourced pink stone. It now functions as a museum hosting art and cultural exhibitions. The palace gardens are carefully maintained and provide a quiet retreat from the surrounding city bustle, with rotating contemporary art displays complementing the historic architecture.

Pakistan Maritime Museum

The Maritime Museum displays decommissioned naval vessels, submarines, and aircraft alongside exhibits covering Pakistan's coastal defence history, offering an unusual hands-on experience for visitors of all ages. The outdoor park area features full-sized aircraft and naval guns, while the indoor galleries trace the evolution of Pakistan's navy from its founding to modern-day peacekeeping missions.
