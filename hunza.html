Hunza Valley

A high-mountain valley framed by some of the world's tallest peaks.

Rakaposhi Viewpoint

Rising to over 7,700 metres, Rakaposhi is one of the most visually striking peaks in the Karakoram range. A dedicated viewpoint along the Karakoram Highway lets visitors take in the mountain's full height from base to summit on a clear day.

Attabad Lake

Attabad Lake formed after a landslide blocked the Hunza River, submerging part of the valley. Today it is a popular spot for boating, with mineral-rich water that takes on a distinctive blue-green colour against the surrounding cliffs.

Baltit Fort

Perched above Karimabad, Baltit Fort served as the residence of Hunza's ruling family for generations. Restored in the 1990s, it now operates as a museum offering panoramic views over the valley floor.
