Swat — Switzerland of Pakistan

Alpine meadows, rivers, and a deep cultural history.

Malam Jabba

Set at an elevation of around 2,800 metres, Malam Jabba is best known for its ski slopes and chairlift, drawing winter sports enthusiasts as well as summer visitors who come for the cool weather and pine-forest views. The resort also features a bowling alley, restaurants, and a small amusement park, making it a year-round destination for families and adventure travellers alike.

Mingora Bazaar

Mingora is the largest town in Swat and its main trading hub. Its bazaars are known for local handicrafts, embroidered textiles, and gemstones sourced from the surrounding mountains. The nearby White Palace, once a royal residence, now serves as a luxury hotel and offers sweeping views of the Swat River valley below.

Swat Museum

The Swat Museum preserves artefacts from the region's Buddhist past, including sculptures and relics from the ancient Gandhara civilisation that once flourished throughout the valley. Located near the historic Butkara Stupa, the museum's collection spans from the second century BCE to the eighth century CE, offering insight into the rich Buddhist heritage that earned Swat the name "Uddiyana" — the garden of ancient Gandhara.
